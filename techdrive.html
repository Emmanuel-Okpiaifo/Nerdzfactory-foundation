TechDrive Program

Bridging the Digital Divide in Secondary Schools through technology education and hands-on training.

About TechDrive Program

The TechDrive Program is an initiative designed to expose secondary school students to technology and digital career opportunities. This program aims to increase digital literacy and skill adoption among young learners, equipping them for the evolving digital economy.

Despite the rapid growth of technology in Nigeria, many students, especially in underserved areas, lack access to digital education. Limited infrastructure, awareness, and opportunities create a digital divide, preventing students from exploring careers in web development, software engineering, and other tech-related fields.

Program Objectives

Through a two-pronged approach, TechDrive will:

  • Raise Awareness – Educate 5,000 students across secondary schools about technology and digital careers
  • Provide Hands-on Training – Equip 100 students with web design skills, enabling them to build their first websites
  • Foster Long-term Engagement – Establish TechDrive Clubs in schools to encourage continued learning

What the Program Covers

  • Tech Awareness & Sensitization – Interactive sessions during school assemblies
  • Web Design Training – Hands-on training in HTML, CSS, and website building fundamentals
  • TechDrive Clubs – School-based clubs to support continuous learning

Program Details

  • Target Audience: Secondary school students (Ages 12-17)
  • Delivery Mode: School-based sensitization and training
  • Duration: Ongoing throughout the academic year
  • Location: Secondary schools across Nigeria
