About the Women Digital Entrepreneurship Program

The Women Digital Entrepreneurship Program (WDEP) seeks to bridge these gaps by equipping women with digital and entrepreneurial skills needed for financial independence. Through expert-led training, mentorship, and hands-on learning, WDEP enables women to start and scale online businesses, improve their digital literacy, and enhance their economic prospects. By empowering women with the right tools, WDEP contributes to building a more inclusive and sustainable digital economy.

The Need for Women's Economic Empowerment

According to the National Bureau of Statistics' 2022 Statistical Report on Women and Men in Nigeria, women face significant economic challenges. In the fourth quarter of 2022, 56% of women were unemployed compared to 44% of men. The unemployment rate for women stood at 6.6%, higher than the 4.4% recorded for men. Additionally, 51.3% of women were out of the labor force, compared to 36.5% of men.

These disparities highlight the barriers women face in accessing stable employment opportunities. Women are also more likely to engage in informal employment, which often lacks stability and benefits. In the second quarter of 2023, 92.7% of employed Nigerians were in informal employment, with the rate being higher among women than men. This indicates the need for structured economic support and skills development programs to help women transition into more sustainable and profitable careers.
